# PART A: INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY ASSIGNMENT AGREEMENT

## 1. Parties

This Intellectual Property Assignment Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of {{DATE}} ("Effective Date") by and between:

**Company:** {{COMPANY_LEGAL_NAME}}  
**Address:** {{COMPANY_ADDRESS}}  
("Company")

and

**Assignor:** _______________________  
**Address:** _______________________  
("Assignor")

---

## 2. Recitals

WHEREAS, Assignor is or will be employed by or engaged as a contractor for Company;

WHEREAS, Company desires to engage Assignor to develop, create, or contribute to certain intellectual property;

WHEREAS, Assignor acknowledges that all Work Product (as defined below) created during their engagement shall be the sole property of Company;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual promises contained herein and other good and valuable consideration, the parties agree as follows:

---

## 3. Definitions

**3.1 "Work Product"** means all inventions, discoveries, ideas, improvements, developments, designs, algorithms, software code, documentation, specifications, trade secrets, processes, techniques, and other intellectual property, whether or not patentable or copyrightable, that Assignor creates, conceives, develops, or reduces to practice, either alone or jointly with others, during the term of Assignor's engagement with Company, that:

(a) Relate to the actual or anticipated business, research, or development of Company;

(b) Result from any work performed by Assignor for Company;

(c) Are developed using Company's equipment, supplies, facilities, or trade secrets; or

(d) Are developed during working hours.

**3.2 "Prior Inventions"** means all inventions, discoveries, developments, and improvements that Assignor made prior to the commencement of engagement with Company that are relevant to Company's business.

**3.3 "Confidential Information"** is defined in Part B of this document.

---

## 4. Assignment of Work Product

### 4.1 Assignment
Assignor hereby irrevocably assigns, transfers, and conveys to Company all right, title, and interest in and to all Work Product, including:

(a) All patents and patent applications;
(b) All copyrights and copyright registrations;
(c) All trade secrets;
(d) All moral rights;
(e) All other intellectual property rights;

in the United States and all foreign countries.

### 4.2 Work Made for Hire
Assignor acknowledges that all Work Product that constitutes copyrightable subject matter shall be considered "work made for hire" as defined by the U.S. Copyright Act, and Company shall be deemed the author thereof. To the extent any Work Product does not qualify as work made for hire, Assignor hereby assigns all copyright rights to Company.

### 4.3 Waiver of Moral Rights
To the extent permitted by applicable law, Assignor hereby irrevocably waives all moral rights in the Work Product, including rights of attribution and integrity.

---

## 5. Disclosure and Cooperation

### 5.1 Disclosure
Assignor agrees to promptly disclose in writing to Company all Work Product developed during the term of engagement.

### 5.2 Cooperation
Assignor agrees to cooperate with Company, at Company's expense, to:

(a) Execute all documents necessary to perfect Company's ownership;
(b) Apply for and obtain patents and copyrights in any country;
(c) Enforce Company's intellectual property rights;
(d) Provide testimony and evidence as needed.

### 5.3 Power of Attorney
Assignor hereby appoints Company as Assignor's attorney-in-fact to execute documents on Assignor's behalf if Assignor is unavailable or refuses to execute such documents.

---

## 6. Prior Inventions

### 6.1 Disclosure of Prior Inventions
Assignor has listed on **Exhibit A** attached hereto all Prior Inventions that Assignor wishes to exclude from this Agreement. If no Prior Inventions are listed, Assignor represents that there are no such Prior Inventions.

### 6.2 License to Prior Inventions
If Assignor incorporates any Prior Invention into any Work Product, Assignor hereby grants to Company a non-exclusive, royalty-free, perpetual, irrevocable, worldwide license to use, modify, and distribute such Prior Invention as part of the Work Product.

---

## 7. Third-Party Rights

### 7.1 No Infringement
Assignor represents and warrants that:

(a) Assignor has the right to make this assignment;
(b) Work Product does not infringe any third-party intellectual property rights;
(c) Assignor has not entered into any agreements that conflict with this Agreement.

### 7.2 Third-Party Materials
Assignor shall not incorporate any third-party materials into Work Product without prior written approval from Company and without ensuring appropriate licenses are in place.

---

## 8. Open Source

### 8.1 Open Source Restrictions
Assignor shall not incorporate any open source software into Work Product without prior written approval from Company, including disclosure of:

(a) The name and version of the open source software;
(b) The applicable open source license;
(c) How the software will be used.

### 8.2 Approved Open Source
Company maintains a list of pre-approved open source licenses and software that may be used without individual approval.

---

## 9. Return of Materials

Upon termination of engagement, Assignor shall:

(a) Return all Company property, including documents, equipment, and materials;
(b) Delete all Company information from personal devices;
(c) Provide access to any Work Product in Assignor's possession;
(d) Certify in writing compliance with this section.

---

## 10. Survival

The obligations under this Part A shall survive termination of Assignor's engagement with Company indefinitely.

---

# PART B: NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT (NDA)

## 11. Confidential Information

### 11.1 Definition
"Confidential Information" means all information disclosed by Company to Assignor, or learned by Assignor during engagement, that is not generally known to the public, including but not limited to:

(a) **Technical Information:** Source code, algorithms, designs, architectures, databases, APIs, specifications, documentation, development tools, and methodologies;

(b) **Business Information:** Business plans, strategies, marketing plans, customer lists, pricing, financial data, and projections;

(c) **Product Information:** Product roadmaps, features, release schedules, and user feedback;

(d) **Personnel Information:** Employee data, compensation, and organizational information;

(e) **Third-Party Information:** Information received from third parties under confidentiality obligations.

### 11.2 Exclusions
Confidential Information does not include information that:

(a) Is or becomes publicly available through no fault of Assignor;
(b) Was known to Assignor before disclosure by Company;
(c) Is independently developed by Assignor without use of Confidential Information;
(d) Is rightfully received from a third party without restriction.

---

## 12. Confidentiality Obligations

### 12.1 Non-Disclosure
Assignor agrees to:

(a) Hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence;
(b) Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent;
(c) Not use Confidential Information except for the benefit of Company;
(d) Take reasonable precautions to prevent unauthorized disclosure.

### 12.2 Permitted Disclosures
Assignor may disclose Confidential Information:

(a) To Company employees and contractors with a need to know;
(b) As required by law or legal process, provided Assignor gives Company prompt notice and cooperates in seeking protective orders.

### 12.3 Standard of Care
Assignor shall protect Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care used to protect Assignor's own confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care.

---

## 13. Non-Compete and Non-Solicitation

### 13.1 Non-Solicitation of Employees
During engagement and for twelve (12) months after termination, Assignor shall not directly or indirectly solicit, recruit, or hire any Company employee or contractor.

### 13.2 Non-Solicitation of Customers
During engagement and for twelve (12) months after termination, Assignor shall not directly or indirectly solicit any Company customer for a competing business.

### 13.3 Non-Compete (Where Enforceable)
During engagement and for [PERIOD - consult local law] after termination, Assignor shall not engage in any business that directly competes with Company's business, to the extent permitted by applicable law.

*Note: Non-compete clauses are unenforceable in some jurisdictions. Consult local counsel.*

---

## 14. Term and Termination

### 14.1 Term
This Agreement shall remain in effect for the duration of Assignor's engagement with Company and shall survive termination as specified herein.

### 14.2 Survival
The confidentiality obligations in Part B shall survive for a period of five (5) years after termination of engagement, except for trade secrets which shall be protected indefinitely.

---

## 15. Remedies

### 15.1 Injunctive Relief
Assignor acknowledges that breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm to Company for which monetary damages would be inadequate. Company shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief without posting bond.

### 15.2 Damages
In addition to injunctive relief, Company may recover actual damages, including lost profits, caused by Assignor's breach.

### 15.3 Attorney's Fees
The prevailing party in any dispute shall be entitled to recover reasonable attorney's fees and costs.

---

## 16. General Provisions

### 16.1 Entire Agreement
This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties concerning its subject matter and supersedes all prior agreements.

### 16.2 Amendment
This Agreement may only be amended by a written document signed by both parties.

### 16.3 Severability
If any provision is found unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in effect.

### 16.4 Governing Law
This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of {{STATE/JURISDICTION}}, without regard to conflicts of law principles.

### 16.5 Assignment
Company may assign this Agreement. Assignor may not assign without Company's written consent.

### 16.6 Notices
All notices shall be in writing and delivered to the addresses set forth above.

---

## 17. Acknowledgment

Assignor acknowledges that:

(a) Assignor has read and understands this Agreement;
(b) Assignor has had the opportunity to consult with legal counsel;
(c) Assignor is signing this Agreement voluntarily;
(d) Assignor agrees to be bound by all terms herein.
